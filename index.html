The Story of Money

Why Bitcoin exists and why it matters

💰 The Problem with Fiat Money

For thousands of years, humans used sound money — gold and silver. These couldn't be created out of thin air.

In 1971, Nixon took the US off the gold standard. Since then, governments print money freely. This is called fiat — from Latin "let it be done".

Result? Your savings lose ~2-7% value every year to inflation. The dollar has lost 97% of its value since 1913.

"In the beginning, there was gold. Then there was paper backed by gold. Then paper backed by nothing. Now... we have Bitcoin."

Why Bitcoin?

The five properties of sound money — Bitcoin has all five

Scarcity 1

Hard Cap: 21 Million

Unlike fiat, Bitcoin's supply is mathematically fixed. No central bank can "create" more. Ever. This is its most important feature.

Durability 2

Never Decays

Gold can be destroyed or seized. Bitcoin exists on thousands of computers worldwide. Back up your seed and it survives anything.

Divisibility 3

Down to Satoshis

One BTC = 100,000,000 satoshis (sats). You can buy $5 worth. No amount is "too small".

Portability 4

Borderless

Send $1 or $1 billion anywhere in the world in minutes. No banks. No borders. No limits.

Verifiability 5

Transparent

Every transaction ever is public. Anyone can verify the 21M supply. No trusted third party needed.

❓ Frequently Asked

Common questions from beginners

Is Bitcoin legal?
Yes! Bitcoin is legal in most countries including the US, EU, Japan, and Canada. It's legal tender in El Salvador and Central African Republic. However, some countries restrict or ban it. Always check your local regulations.
How do I get started?
1) Learn first (you're in the right place!). 2) Get a wallet. 3) Buy small amounts (DCA). 4) Never share your seed phrase. 5) Hold for the long term. Start with what you can afford to lose.
What's a seed phrase?
Your seed phrase (12 or 24 words) is your master key. Write it down ON PAPER. Store it somewhere safe. Anyone with this phrase can access your funds. NEVER store digitally. NEVER share with anyone.
Why is Bitcoin volatile?
Young markets are volatile. Bitcoin is only 16 years old. As adoption grows, volatility decreases. Remember: gold was volatile too in its early years. The trend is up over time.
What about the energy usage?
Bitcoin uses energy to secure the network. But it's often waste energy (flared gas, hydro overflows). It actually incentivizes renewable energy development. More importantly, traditional banking uses MORE energy when you count ATMs, branches, servers, etc.
What determines Bitcoin's price?
Supply and demand. Only 21M will ever exist. Demand comes from: adoption, scarcity narrative, institutional buying, halving events. Price is simply where supply meets demand at any moment.
