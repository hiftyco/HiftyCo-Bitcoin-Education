What is Money?

Before you can understand Bitcoin, you must first understand what makes money... money.

📖 Definition

Money is anything generally accepted as a medium of exchange, a store of value, and a unit of account. Throughout history, humans have used shells, gold, silver, and paper as money.

💸

Fiat Currency

Government-issued paper with no intrinsic value. Backed by "trust" in governments.

  • Infinite supply (governments can print)
  • Loses 3-7% value yearly to inflation
  • Can be frozen or seized
  • Requires banks as middlemen
  • 24/7 printing presses
₿

Bitcoin

The world's first decentralized digital currency. Money without masters.

  • Fixed 21 million supply (mathematical)
  • Deflationary by design
  • Censorship resistant
  • Peer-to-peer (no middlemen)
  • Halves every ~4 years

What is Bitcoin?

Bitcoin (₿) is the world's first successful decentralized cryptocurrency, launched in 2009 by a mysterious creator known as Satoshi Nakamoto. It operates on a peer-to-peer network, meaning users can send payments directly to each other without banks or governments.

Bitcoin can be divided into 100,000,000 satoshis (sats), allowing for micro-payments. It's often called "digital gold" because, like gold, it's scarce and serves as a store of value. The ticker symbol is BTC.

How Bitcoin Works

Three components work together to create decentralized money

1

Blockchain

A public, distributed ledger that records all transactions. Immutable and transparent.

2

Proof of Work

Miners compete to validate transactions using computing power. Secures the network.

3

Decentralization

Thousands of nodes worldwide. No central authority. Everyone can participate.

4

Cryptography

Public/private key pairs secure ownership. Your keys, your coins.

🔑 Double-Spending Problem

Before Bitcoin, digital money had a fundamental problem: how do you prevent someone from spending the same coin twice? Banks solve this with centralized ledgers. Bitcoin solves this with thousands of copies of the same ledger — the entire network must agree on every transaction. This is called consensus.

5 Properties of Sound Money

What makes Bitcoin better than fiat

🎯

1. Scarcity — 21 Million

Bitcoin's supply is mathematically fixed at 21 million. No central bank can create more. This is guaranteed by the code itself. Unlike gold (which can be discovered) or fiat (which can be printed), Bitcoin's scarcity is absolute.

🛡️

2. Censorship Resistance

No government can freeze your funds. No bank can block transactions. Once you have the keys, only you can move your bitcoin. This is true financial sovereignty — your wealth can't be confiscated.

🌍

3. Borderless & Fast

Send $1 or $1 billion anywhere in the world in minutes. No bank delays, no border restrictions, no SWIFT fees. With Lightning Network, payments are instant for everyday use.

⚡

4. Divisibility

One BTC = 100,000,000 satoshis (sats). You can buy $5 worth. No amount is too small. This enables micro-payments and financial access for billions of unbanked people.

🔐

5. Self-Custody

Be your own bank. Your private keys, your bitcoin. Not your keys, not your coins. With hardware wallets, your wealth is secured by physics, not promises.
